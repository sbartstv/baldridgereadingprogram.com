About the Baldridge Reading and Study Strategy Program

A historic 70-year reading and study strategy program taught at over 400 Ivy League universities, private universities, colleges, private country day schools, Christian, Catholic, Hebrew, and military schools worldwide.

Est. 1956 — Greenwich, Connecticut

Baldridge Reading and Study Strategy Services was founded in Greenwich, Connecticut in 1956 by Kenneth P. Baldridge and a staff drawn from Ivy League universities. Over seven decades, the program was taught to more than 100,000 students across 400+ private schools, colleges, and universities worldwide.

Clifford P. Baldridge continues the family legacy as Co-Director of Baldridge Reading — West Coast Branch, serving Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, Malibu, and Santa Barbara in person, and students worldwide via Zoom.

Taught at The White House

The Baldridge family was invited to The White House, where the program was taught to the President's Staff to refine the collection and filtering of White House Mail and Global Intelligence — including revision of the President's Daily Brief (PDB), the top-secret document delivered each morning to the President of the United States.

"We greatly appreciate the program conducted by your staff. It was professionally conducted, your instructors were outstanding and the participants' remarks have been very complimentary in every way."

— Landon Kite, Director of Correspondence, The White House

Johns Hopkins University — 24 Years

"Johns Hopkins Academic Dean for Academic Studies Martha O. Roseman from the office of Academic Advising endorsed the initiation of the Baldridge Reading Program at Hopkins."

The Baldridge Program was taught to thousands of students and graduate Teaching Assistants at Johns Hopkins for over 11 years. Below is the historic newsletter article from the Johns Hopkins University announcing the endorsement.

From the Johns Hopkins University Newsletter. "Office of Advising Endorses Baldridge Reading Program" by Patrick P. Lynch. Martha O. Roseman, former Associate Dean of Academic Advising at The Johns Hopkins University. Historical reconstruction.

International Excellence

The Baldridge Program has been taught internationally in Brussels, Munich, Vienna, Las Palmas, and Tokyo — and aboard the S.S. Universe between mainland China and Sri Lanka as part of a Semester at Sea voyage around the world.

Corporate and government clients include General Electric, IBM, Time-Warner, J.P. Morgan & Co., Alcan, Reader's Digest, and the U.S. Department of Labor.
