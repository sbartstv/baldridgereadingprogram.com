About the Baldridge Program

A 70-year family legacy in reading and study skills education, taught at 400+ schools and universities worldwide.

Est. 1956 — Greenwich, Connecticut

Baldridge Reading and Study Strategy Services was founded in Greenwich, Connecticut in 1956 by Kenneth P. Baldridge and a staff drawn from Ivy League universities. Over seven decades, the program was taught to more than 100,000 students across 400+ private schools, colleges, and universities.

Clifford P. Baldridge continues the family legacy as Co-Director of Baldridge Reading — West Coast Branch, serving Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, Malibu, and Santa Barbara, and students worldwide via Zoom.

Taught at The White House

The Baldridge family was invited to The White House, where the program was taught to the President's Staff to refine collection and filtering of White House Mail and Global Intelligence — including revision of the President's Daily Brief (PDB), the top-secret document delivered each morning to the President of the United States.

"We greatly appreciate the program conducted by your staff. It was professionally conducted, your instructors were outstanding and the participants' remarks have been very complimentary in every way."

— Landon Kite, Director of Correspondence, The White House

Johns Hopkins University — 24 Years

"Johns Hopkins Academic Dean for Academic Studies Martha O. Roseman from the office of Academic Advising endorsed the initiation of the Baldridge Reading Program at Hopkins."

International Excellence

The Baldridge Program has been taught in Brussels, Munich, Vienna, Las Palmas, and Tokyo — and aboard the S.S. Universe between mainland China and Sri Lanka as part of a Semester at Sea voyage around the world.

Corporate and government clients include General Electric, IBM, Time-Warner, J.P. Morgan & Co., Alcan, Reader's Digest, and the U.S. Department of Labor.
